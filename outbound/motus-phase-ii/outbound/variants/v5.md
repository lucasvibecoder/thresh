**Send window:** Tue 2026-05-12 or Wed 2026-05-13
**Word count target:** 75–100
**Variant axis:** lead with the "default risk reads as" reframing → trigger → stat → CTA
**Stat surface form:** `Twenty percent of carriers operating under active FMCSA authority...`

```
default risk early warning
```

## Body

```
{{first_name}} - At a factoring desk, default risk usually reads as a

Lucas
```

## Failure-mode scan

- **Target/register:** factoring vocab front-loaded ("factoring desk," "default risk," "missed payment," "recovery call," "your book"). Strongest register fit of any variant. ✓
- **AI-SDR pattern:** no LinkedIn quote. ✓
- **Forced analogies:** "reads as" is colloquial, not analogical. ✓
- **Telescoping:** general-pattern claim ("default risk usually reads as"), not one-company. ✓
- **Machinery before curiosity:** one line of what I do. ✓
- **Jargon:** all recipient vocab. ✓
- **Dual CTA:** single. ✓
- **Premature attachments:** none. ✓
- **Reframing-led risk:** opener is the boldest of the five — could read as preachy if recipient resists the frame. Mitigated by it being factually neutral ("usually reads as" not "is supposed to read as"), and the second sentence acknowledges the standard outcome rather than claiming superiority. ✓
- **"Earliest public signal" overclaim:** softened from "earliest possible signal" to "earliest public signal" — FMCSA's internal scoring could see things sooner; this scopes the claim to public-data. ✓

## Decisions intentionally NOT taken

- **No "in my experience" or "I've seen" framing** — v5 is system-level, not consultant-anecdote register.
- **No mention of competitive monitoring tools** ("most credit tools surface this too late") — that veers into competitive comparison which dilutes the trigger urgency.
- **"Twenty percent" spelled out** instead of "20%" — surface-form rotation against v1's "20%" rendering.
