**Word count target:** 60–75 (intentionally shortest variant)
**Variant axis:** every sentence single-thought, no second-clause anywhere, raw-count stat
**Stat surface form:** `Over 130,000 active-authority carriers...` (raw count from methodology — 136,498)

## Methodology citation (use only if asked)

136,498 of 680,056 active-authority carriers have MCS-150 dates older than 24 months — same FMCSA Company Census methodology, raw-count expression instead of percentage.

## Failure-mode scan

- **Target/register:** factoring vocab ("default risk," "your book"). ✓
- **AI-SDR pattern:** none. ✓
- **Forced analogies:** none. ✓
- **Telescoping:** raw count is system-level (not "Carrier X has a stale MCS-150"). ✓
- **Machinery before curiosity:** one line. ✓
- **Jargon:** all in recipient vocab. ✓
- **Dual CTA:** single. ✓
- **Premature attachments:** none. ✓
- **Stripped-variant risk:** could feel curt / robotic at 60 words. Mitigated by leaving the "Want me to show you?" CTA conversational and the "early warning on default risk before it hits your book" framing keeping the why visible. ✓

## Decisions intentionally NOT taken

- **No "the most common of these gaps" qualifier** — v1's framing got trimmed entirely to keep single-thought rule.
- **No paragraph break before the CTA** in many cold emails — kept the break here so the eye lands on the question.
- **Raw count over percentage** — 130,000+ feels more visceral than 20%. Picked specifically because v1, v2, v3, v5 all render the percentage form; v4 is the only count-form variant for anti-clustering.
