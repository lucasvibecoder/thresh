Core Principles

Three rules behind every play.

01_

Public substrate first.

Crunchbase funding announcements, LinkedIn exec moves, PESP monthly PE healthcare M&A reports, OIG LEIE monthly CSV, SEC EDGAR full-text search, compact-membership registries — anchored on facts a recipient can look up. No "we work with platforms like yours" as the lead.

02_

Lead with the trigger, not the pitch.

Name the round size, the state target, the bolt-on entity, the new exec's start date. Product lands as the logical response after the recipient agrees the situation is real — not before.

03_

Operator voice, not vendor voice.

"The math compounds fast," not "scalable enterprise solutions." "Per-payer enrollment runs 90-120 days," not "AI-powered automation reduces friction." COO, not "ops stakeholder." The role-play section below is where the vendor voice got debugged.

Executive Context

Why this market, why now.

Assured builds AI-native provider credentialing, payer enrollment, licensing, and network management for digital-health and PE-backed multi-site groups. $6M Seed (October 2025, led by First Round Capital with Kindred Ventures and the founders of Atria and Grow Therapy), NCQA-certified as a Credentials Verification Organization, and growing 30% month-over-month with named customers including Blossom Health, Tono Health, Birches Health, SOL Mental Health, Platinum Dermatology Partners, and Houston Methodist.

The strategic moment: the buyer side is hit simultaneously by Joint Commission's January 2026 SAFER-matrix standards (automated audit trails, traceable peer-review documentation), NCQA's tightened recredentialing timeframes, climbing payer denials (15-17% in 2026 commercial + Medicare Advantage), and a digital-health Series B/C funding wave deploying capital into multi-state expansion plans that credentialing throughput cannot meet at industry-baseline 60-120 day per-payer enrollment cycles. Meanwhile the competitive substrate has shifted — Medallion just raised $43M and launched CredAlliance (a national credentialing clearinghouse) in August 2025, putting capital asymmetry pressure on Assured to defend its segment niche before Medallion's payer-side product gains traction.

Almost none of these buyers are actively shopping for credentialing software. The strongest signals here are operational moments that force a credentialing-system review against an external clock — and those moments are exactly what this playbook detects.

Deep 01_

Series B/C with state-expansion math gap

Failure mode: Multi-state expansion gated on credentialing throughput. Capital deployed on a board-committed expansion timeline; per-payer enrollment math will slip that timeline by 4-6 months even when the credentialing team is fully staffed. The COO doesn't know this when the round closes; they will, 60-90 days in.

Trigger · Series B or C funding announcement in the last 90 days where press-release language explicitly references state-by-state expansion (≥3 US state names, or phrases like "national rollout," "expand to N states").

Persona · COO / Head of Operations at the funded org (50-1,000 employees, multi-state expansion mandate fresh from board).

Disqualifiers · Round announced 91+ days ago; provider count >2,000; recent vendor partnership disclosed in same announcement (e.g., "partnered with Medallion").

PVP · One-page per-state-per-payer enrollment-timeline mapping for the prospect's announced state targets, flagging which payers in their top 2 expansion states are the longest pole. Useful even if they never buy — most ops teams haven't built this view themselves.

Competitive Landscape

Where Assured wins, where it loses, where the gaps are.

Where Assured wins

  • → Series A-C digital-health platforms scaling multi-state who need both speed AND audit-ready compliance (NCQA CVO is the differentiator)
  • → Founder-led pre-Series-A digital health where the cap-table angel network (Atria, Grow Therapy founders) generates warm-intro flow
  • → Buyers actively frustrated with Medallion's reported support gaps, errors, rigid workflows, and pricing tiers gating automation
  • → PE-backed multi-site groups doing M&A integrations where 50-5,000-provider scaling without workflow change is a deal-thesis match
  • → Behavioral-health digital platforms working through PSYPACT / Counseling Compact state activations where the payer-enrollment math lags the licensure math
  • → Mid-stage orgs that have outgrown a single credentialing manager but can't justify Symplr's enterprise contract

Where Assured loses

  • → Symplr-embedded enterprise IDNs where switching cost dwarfs speed gains (12-18-month sales cycles, 9-of-10 US hospitals already using Symplr)
  • → Verifiable-API-first technical buyers who want raw API access and have engineering to build their own UX
  • → Buyers waiting for Medallion CredAlliance payer-side traction before switching
  • → Capital-asymmetry losses: $6M seed vs. Medallion's $130M means Assured can't outspend on enterprise sales firepower
  • → Single-state stable orgs without expansion pressure — credentialing throughput isn't an existential question for them yet

Gaps Assured can own

  • → Compact-membership additions (Play 7) — none of Medallion / Symplr / Verifiable / CertifyOS appear to monitor compact-state activations as a trigger; thin-volume, high-leverage
  • → PE bolt-on + health-system M&A targeting (Plays 3, 8) — most credentialing vendors don't connect PE deal-flow to outbound; Assured's behavioral-health and PE customer base gives reference credibility
  • → Vendor-complaint signal (Play 5) — Assured's competitor-comparison content library is purpose-built for this funnel; direct landing-page fit
  • → OIG LEIE peer events (Play 9) — compliance-officer outreach from peer-pressure events is largely uncontested in the credentialing-vendor space
  • → Customer-base aggregated benchmark productization — once Assured packages FTE-to-provider ratios, FPAR by state, and denial-rate-by-payer into a prospect-facing artifact, Plays 2 and 4 cross from Cat B to Cat A primary

What This Playbook Doesn't Solve

Where this approach falls short.

  • No Cat A primary plays — Assured has aggregated benchmark data across hundreds of customer orgs (FPAR by state, cred-turnaround by specialty, denial-rate by payer) but hasn't yet packaged it into a prospect-facing artifact. Once it does, Plays 2 and 4 cross to Cat A primary.
  • Enterprise-IDN segment underserved at the top tier — Joint Commission survey window (Play 6) ships but the 12-18-month sales cycle exceeds what $6M-seed-stage GTM firepower can carry. Houston Methodist is Assured's lone large-IDN logo; until that pattern repeats, IDN is a beachhead, not a focus motion.
  • Vendor-complaint signal (Play 5) requires identity enrichment that's hard at scale — Reddit usernames + Capterra anonymized reviews don't always trace to a named org. Limits play to ~50-200 high-conviction events/year vs. larger raw signal volume.
  • Joint Commission Quality Check has no documented public API — Play 6 detection requires a Playwright headless scrape; no clean structured endpoint. Medium-feasibility automation, not high.
  • Public-co credentialing receivable disclosure (Play 10) is rare — 5-15 high-conviction events per year. High-value but low-volume; doesn't carry sustained pipeline by itself.
  • Medallion CredAlliance unknown unknowns — if Medallion's payer-side product gains material traction with major commercial payers, the buyer's "wait and see" objection in Play 6 gets stronger. Pre-empt with a CredAlliance-positioning section by Q3 if needed.
