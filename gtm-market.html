Methodology

How this was built.

Every job description was forced into the same structured schema — 40+ fields per JD, normalized against a canonical tool list, classified into role archetypes, and cross-referenced for patterns. This isn't a ChatGPT summary. It's a structured data pipeline with a processing harness that gets smarter with every JD it ingests.

01 — Ingest

JDs sourced from job boards, Clay enrichment, and direct company pages. Raw text preserved, never modified.

02 — Parse

Each JD parsed into YAML frontmatter — company, title, tools, skills, archetype, salary, stage, and more. Tool names normalized to canonical list.

03 — Analyze

Analysis regenerated from structured data. Tool frequency, skill tiers, salary bands, archetype clusters — all computed, not hand-written.

Key Finding

The market is shifting toward GTM Systems Architect as the dominant archetype (33% of JDs). Companies want GTM Engineers who can own the full stack, not just one channel. Outbound Builder remains the most accessible entry point (30%), but the growth trajectory points toward systems architecture — and the $18k salary premium reflects it.

Key Finding

The skill that separates good GTM Engineers from great ones isn't a tool — it's AI/LLM fluency (56% of JDs). Companies don't just want you to use AI tools. They want you to build AI-native workflows, write prompts, and deploy agents. The second biggest differentiator is API/integration depth (48%) — connecting systems, not just configuring them.
